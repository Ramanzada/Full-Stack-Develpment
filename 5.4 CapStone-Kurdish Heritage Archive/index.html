EST. 2026 / VOLUME I / FOUR LANDS, ONE PEOPLE

A people without a state, never without a memory.

The Kurdish Heritage Archive is a record of a nation divided across four borders — Bakur, Bashur, Rojhelat, and Rojava — bound by language, mountain, and the long refusal to be forgotten.

01 — About

What this archive holds.

History written by empires has long treated the Kurds as a footnote — a minority, a problem, a borderland. This archive begins from the opposite premise. Here, the Kurdish story is the centre, not the margin.

The collection draws together timelines, biographies, photographs, and primary sources spanning from the Mahabad Republic to the present day. Each entry is written in plain language and cited where possible.
